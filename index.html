NeuroMooves (movement & body awareness)

Warm-up: Spatial & body awareness exercises.

Movements: "Side Step Left, Side Step Right", "Turn Head Left and Right", "4 Swings Monkey Hanging in a Tree" (slow & fast music) · "Marching Knees and Arms High", "Catching Stars", "Clap 4 times" (follow the leader) · "Vegetable Messages", "Jump and Count" · "Spontaneous Clap Slap Stomp".

Obstacle course (basic gymnastics): Dog with a Broken Leg, Bunny Hop, Crocodile Crawl, Frog Jump, Follow the GPS, Jumping for Height, Trampoline, Jump over a Marker, Leap and Land like a Dinosaur, Double-sided Vault, Log Roll, Egg Roll, Cylindrical Log, Forward Roll.

Relaxation moves: Apple, Carrot, Basket, Broccoli, Banana, Orange.

Creative visualisation: Growing Yummy Food; Trying a New Fruit!

Creativity & Theatrical Strategies

Impulse exercises: The Blowing & Growing Seeds Game; The Insects in the Garden; Growing as a Circle of Sunflowers; Growing into Sunflowers.

Physical becoming exercises: The Tiny Seed's Journey; Growing Plants; The Bees & the Different Coloured Pollen; Blow, Plant, Grow, Weed; Becoming a Sunflower House; The Pollinating Race; The Blowing & Growing Seeds Game; Blowing & Growing as a Pod of Beans.

Divergent thinking exercises: Moving like People, Growing like Plants; Plants with Different Qualities; Making Houses from Plants; Animal Pollen Carrying Race; Growing into Different Plants.

Numeracy, enquiry learning & gourmet moments

Numeracy strategies: Five interwoven strands are covered across the year — Patterns and Function, Numbers, Shapes and Space, Measurement, and Data Handling. Each week's integrated activities may touch more than one strand.

Enquiry teaching & learning: Integrated across numeracy, language, creative art & craft, gourmet moments, music & movement — tailored to what the children already know and what they're curious about. Teachers share the specifics in the Curriculum Updates.

Gourmet moments: Multisensory cooking/tasting activities planned around the "Growing Things" theme, incorporating sensory language.
